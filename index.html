About Me

I'm a fourth-year PhD student at the University of California, Berkeley, where I'm advised by Krste Asanović. Currently, I'm working on the Gemmini project. Gemmini is a platform that we're building for full-system, full-stack evaluation of DNN accelerator workloads. In the past, I've also contributed to the Chisel hardware construction language, and to MAVBench, an end-to-end benchmarking platform for drone applications.

My research interests include the design of hardware accelerators for DNN workloads, as well as the design of programming interfaces for such accelerators. I am also interested in agile hardware design methodologies.
